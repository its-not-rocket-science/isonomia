# isonomia

**A framework for modelling the emergence, resilience, and dissolution of governance systems**

*Named for the pre-Athenian concept of equal political arrangement — closer to what the historical record shows most human societies sought than the later vocabulary of democracy or liberty.*

---

## Overview

This repository contains the data, code, and paper for a theoretical framework unifying two currently separate bodies of work:

- **Emergence models** — how governance forms arise, centred on Graeber & Wengrow's *The Dawn of Everything* (2021)
- **Collapse models** — why complex hierarchical societies fail, centred on Turchin's secular cycle theory and Tainter's marginal returns framework

The central contribution is that **the three Graeber-Wengrow freedoms** (exit, disobedience, arrangement) function as a **composite resilience indicator** bridging the emergence and collapse literatures through a unified phase-space model.

## The Model

### SAP Phase Space — three elementary forms of domination (Graeber & Wengrow)
- **S** Sovereignty — concentration of coercive power (0–1)
- **A** Administration — bureaucratic/record-keeping apparatus (0–1)
- **P** Competitive Politics — prestige/heroic/performative politics (0–1)

### EDR Resilience Composite — three Graeber-Wengrow freedoms
- **E** Exit — can members leave the system? (0–1)
- **D** Disobedience — can authority be refused? (0–1)
- **R** Arrangement — can new social forms be created? (0–1)

### Core Hypothesis
*Societies where (E+D+R)/3 exceeds a threshold θ retain self-correcting capacity; those below θ enter a fragile regime where Turchin/Tainter collapse dynamics become operative.*

### Key Findings (preliminary)
- EDR–SAP correlation: **r = −0.844** (hand-coded subset, n = 125); **r = −0.775** (full dataset, n = 389)
- 42% of systems fall below the resilience threshold θ = 0.45
- The EDR distribution is **bimodal** — systems cluster above or below θ with few in between, consistent with a genuine phase boundary rather than a smooth gradient
- The timeline shows **no secular decline in EDR** — high-resilience societies appear at every period, directly supporting the G&W argument against stadial inevitability

## Dataset

`governance_extended.csv` contains 389 governance systems spanning approximately 12,000 BCE to the present. It merges and extends two upstream sources:

- [`global-governance-models`](https://github.com/its-not-rocket-science/global-governance-models) — 331 real historical systems with rich descriptive columns
- Theory-driven additions (58 systems) targeting G&W-critical cases: early urbanism, Fertile Crescent pre-state forms, egalitarian large-scale societies, and SAP phase-space anchor cases

**Coding confidence:**
- `3` = Direct archaeological/textual evidence for the specific variables
- `2` = Reasonable inference from known structural features
- `1` = Auto-coded from governance type/characteristics — requires human review

125 systems are hand-coded at confidence 2–3. 264 are auto-coded at confidence 1 and flagged for review. The hand-coded subset is the basis for all quantitative claims in the paper.

**New in Phase 3:** 5 targeted `succession_changes` additions (Teotihuacan, Tiv, Igbo, Andean Tinku, Mossi Naam) raising type-changing transitions from 26 to 31; `survival_spells.csv` generated (145 spells, 31 events, 102,214 system-years).

## Relationship to Upstream Data

`isonomia` uses and extends `global-governance-models` but does not modify it. The source dataset is upstream; this repository adds the theoretical annotation layer and paper-specific cases.

## Cross-Validation

The EDR composite and SAP variables have been validated against five independent external datasets. None are redistributed in the repository — place downloaded files in `downloads/` and run:

```bash
python src/crossvalidate_edr.py \
    --vdem   downloads/V-Dem-CY-Core-v16.csv \
    --polity downloads/p5v2018d.xls \
    --seshat downloads/Equinox2020.05.2023.csv \
    --wjp    downloads/2025_wjp_rule_of_law_index_HISTORICAL_DATA_FILE.xlsx \
    --fiw    downloads/All_data_FIW_2013-2021.xlsx \
    --ccp    downloads/ccpcnc_v5.zip
```

| Dataset | Download | Key result |
|---|---|---|
| V-Dem v16 (Coppedge et al. 2026) | https://www.v-dem.net | EDR vs libdem r = 0.868–0.912*** (n = 16) |
| Polity5 (Marshall & Gurr 2020) | https://www.systemicpeace.org/inscrdata.html | EDR vs DEMOC r = 0.847***, SAP vs AUTOC r = 0.895*** (n = 11) |
| WJP Rule of Law Index 2025 | https://worldjusticeproject.org/rule-of-law-index/ | E r = 0.904**, R r = 0.861* (n = 7 direct) |
| Freedom House FIW 2013–2021 | https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-world | EDR vs civil liberties r = 0.923*** (n = 9 direct) |
| CCP Comparative Constitutions v5 | https://comparativeconstitutionsproject.org/download-data/ | D vs constitutional constraints r = 0.681* (n = 11; de jure) |
| Seshat Equinox-2020 | https://seshat-db.com/downloads_page/ | SAP vs admin/military r = 0.604–0.774** (n = 14 pre-modern) |

Full results and matched comparison data: `data/crossval_matched.csv`. See `data/coding_scheme.md` for complete tables including the CCP de jure/de facto gap analysis.
